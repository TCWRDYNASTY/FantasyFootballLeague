Constitution

1 Entry and Fees:

a) $25 buy-in fee.
b) $25 buyout fee if they decide to leave.
c) Buyout money will be added to next year’s winning pot.

2 Winning Pot Breakdown:

a) First place receives $205.
b) Second place receives $50.
c) Third place receives $25.
d) $70 from the pot will go toward trophy maintenance. This covers engraving, shipping to the current winner, and nameplate updates. Any remaining funds will go to the previous year’s champion.

*Buyout fee will be split evenly between first, second, and third.

3 League size:

14 teams, no divisions.

4 Playoffs:

a) 6-team playoff.
b) First and second get first-round byes.
c) Highest seed plays lowest seed.
d) Playoffs begin Week 15.

5 Trade Deadline:

a) Trade deadline is Week 11.
b) No trade vetoes — all accepted trades will be processed.
c) Trades will be reviewed and processed within 1 day.

6 Waivers:

Rolling continuous waivers.

7 New League Year:

a) The trade window opens at the start of the NFL league year, usually in March.
b) The rookie draft will begin one day after the NFL Draft and will take place over the weekend, with adjustable pick times.
c) Free agents can be added 1 week after the rookie draft.

9 Rookie Draft:

a) Three-round linear draft.
b) NBA-style weighted draft order: Teams ranked 7–14 will be entered into a lottery for the first overall pick, with the highest odds going to the team that finishes in last place. Teams ranked 1–6 will be placed at the back of the draft and out of the lottery, with the 1st place team receiving the 14th overall pick.
c) Draft order will be released Super Bowl weekend.
d) Odds:

14 = 21%
13 = 18%
12 = 16%
11 = 14%
10 = 12%
9 = 8%
8 = 6%
7 = 5%

10 Roster:

1 Starting QB
2 Starting RB
2 Starting WR
1 Starting TE
1 Starting Flex (TE/WR/RB)
1 Starting Defense
1 Starting Kicker
10 Bench Spots
3 Taxi Spots
4 IR Spots

11 Taxi Squad:

Rookies with less than one year of experience are eligible. They can be placed in taxi squad spots at any point during the season.
